## HOAs
To grow Oaktree Landscaping’s commercial portfolio, you should target two tiers of HOAs: the High-End Gated Communities (Plantations) that require "white-glove" amenity management, and the High-Density Residential Developments that offer consistent, high-volume maintenance opportunities.

### The "Big Seven" Luxury Communities (High-Margin)
These are large-scale, private gated communities with massive common areas, clubhouses, and private roads. They typically have their own on-site management or use premium external firms.
* Sea Pines Resort (Hilton Head): The largest on the island; requires high-frequency maintenance for world-class tourism standards.
* Palmetto Bluff (Bluffton): A 20,000-acre community. They prioritize "low-impact" and native landscaping (perfect for your Native Planting 2026 pitch).
* Hilton Head Plantation (Hilton Head): One of the most established with over 4,000 homes and extensive common property.
* Sun City Hilton Head (Bluffton/Okatie): An massive 55+ community with thousands of homes. They manage multiple clubhouses and fitness centers.
* Hampton Lake (Bluffton): Known for its "lakeside village" and extensive water-side landscaping needs.
* Berkeley Hall (Bluffton): A premier golf community with high standards for turf quality and floral rotations.
* Colleton River Club (Bluffton): Features two championship courses and requires meticulous estate-level maintenance for common areas.

## Strategic Bluffton Neighborhoods (High Volume)
These communities often have "regime" setups for townhomes or specific sections where the HOA pays for 100% of the landscaping, rather than individual homeowners.
* The Farm at Buckwalter: Large neighborhood with extensive community trails and parks.
* Bluffton Park: High-density area near Old Town with significant common area turf and streetscapes.
* Hampton Hall: A private community with significant common-area maintenance needs and carriage home regimes.
* Pinecrest: A golf community where the entryways and common grounds are a high priority for the board.
* Lawton Station: A gated community in the "New Riverside" corridor with growing maintenance requirements.
* Westbury Park: Known for its "Charleston-style" architecture and high-visibility community squares.


## Local Economic Gatekeepers
Joining these organizations provides direct access to the decision-makers at the companies listed above.
* BOMA Georgia, BOMA Greater Charleston, & BOMA South Carolina: The Building Owners and Managers Association is where regional facility managers network.
* Charleston Metro Chamber of Commerce: Specifically their "Property Management" and "Real Estate" circles.
* Savannah Area Chamber of Commerce: Target their "Developer" and "Property Management" member segments.
* Hilton Head Island-Bluffton Chamber: Critical for resort and HOA management contracts.
* IFMA (International Facility Management Association) - South Carolina Chapter: Covers Charleston and Hilton Head, Georgia Chapter: Covers Savannah and surrounding industrial hubs.
* IREM (Institute of Real Estate Management)
* *NALP (National Association of Landscape Professionals) - The primary national body. They offer the "Navigate Business Mentoring Program" which can help you scale Oaktree from a local player to a regional commercial powerhouse.
* SC Green (South Carolina Green Industry Association) - This is the most important regional body for the Lowcountry. Their annual SC Green Conference & Trade Show (held every January) is the best place to find local suppliers and see what Savannah/Charleston competitors are doing. Encourage your team to become Certified Horticultural Professionals (CHP) through SC Green to boost your "Expert" brand.
* Georgia Urban Ag Council (UAC) - For your Savannah operations, the UAC provides legislative advocacy and specific networking for the Georgia coastal region.
* ULI (Urban Land Institute) - ULI is where the developers go. If you want to get into the "Design-Build" phase of new commercial office parks or shopping centers, you need to be at ULI South Carolina or ULI Coastal Georgia events.
